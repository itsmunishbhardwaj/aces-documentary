# Documentary Roles

6-month applied AI cohort documentary. 30 remote subjects.

| Role | What the role entails | Person |
|---|---|---|
| **Director** | Author of the film. Decides what the documentary *is* — the story, tone, point of view, and what each scene needs to feel like. Directs interviews, gives the editor creative direction, signs off on the final cut. | Munish |
| **Producer** | Operational owner. Recruits and onboards the 30 subjects, secures signed appearance releases, schedules across time zones, hires and pays crew, owns the budget and timeline, single point of contact for subjects, keeps everyone unblocked. | Hansh. Sagar? |
| **DP (Remote)** | Owns the visual standard. Specs the self-shoot kit (camera/phone, lights, mic), writes the self-shoot guide, runs onboarding sessions with subjects, on-call for technical troubleshooting, ensures 30 separate camera operators produce footage that cuts together. | Chris? |
| **Story Producer** | Finds the narrative. With 30 subjects, spots arcs early, decides which 5–8 students to follow deeply, surfaces threads and themes for the editor, maintains the story bible across 6 months of footage. | Shefali/Eddie/Hansh |
| **Editor** | Cuts the film. Assembles footage into scenes, sequences, and a feature. Works closely with Director and Story Producer. Owns pacing, structure, and rhythm. | Me/Eddie? |
| **Audio Lead** | Sound design + original score in one. Cleans up dialogue, designs ambience, mixes the final film, composes original music. Needs proven reels in both disciplines. | Om? |
| **Distribution** | Gets the film in front of audiences. Festival strategy, platform deals (YouTube/streamer/theatrical), marketing, press, release timing. Often engaged later in the project. | Yash |
