## PROTAGONIST SELECTION PROCESS

**Two-stage system — do not collapse into one stage:**

```
Stage 1 → Questionnaire to all 30 → self-select interest via tick box
Stage 2 → Munish + Shefali apply rubric → shortlist 8–10 → deep questionnaire
         → Munish selection conversations (15 min each, Ch. 13)
         → 5 protagonists confirmed
         → Before Interviews M2 Week 1 (Ch. 13A) — DO NOT SKIP
```

**Selection rubric (max 20 points):**

| Criterion | Max | Look For |
|---|---|---|
| Specificity | 5 | Real, personal answers — not polished or generic |
| Emotional Stakes | 5 | Something genuinely at risk — not just a learning goal |
| Contrast Potential | 5 | Fills a gap in the ensemble — age, country, archetype |
| Surprise Factor | 5 | At least one answer that stopped you |

**Thresholds:** Shortlist: 14+ · Discuss with Munish: 10–13 · Pass: below 10

### 🟣 Shefali Mody — Working Agreement

**My role:** Showrunner / Narrator / Story Producer / PM (temp)

**My time zone & availability:**
California (PT). I work late — typically available 10:00pm–3:00am PT, which overlaps well with India morning time. Don't be surprised by late-night messages. Don't feel obligated to match my hours.

**My priorities (so you understand where this project sits):**
1. Claude Architect Certification
2. Separate coaching program — sits above 100X
3. The documentary — I genuinely consider this fun and it is a real priority
4. Projects with Eddie (ongoing)
5. Client coaching load — contained within time blocks, doesn't bleed into discretionary time

**How I like to receive feedback:**
Direct, honest, and one-on-one. Come to me privately — not in the group chat. The sooner after something happens the better. Say the thing plainly. I can handle it.

**How I like to give feedback:**
The same way. Directly, privately, as close to the moment as possible. You'll hear it from me — not through someone else, not in a group meeting.

**My working style:**
I think out loud and move fast. I'll have five ideas before breakfast. I also swirl — if you see me going in circles, call it out. I make better decisions when options are in front of me rather than still in exploration mode.

**Communication preference:**
Brevity over explanation. If I say "got it" — I've got it. Move on. Don't give me a five-minute explanation to get something out of your system. If you want to go deeper on something, ask me a question. I'll tell you if I need more.

**When I'm stuck or overwhelmed:**
Help me prioritise, don't add to the list. If you see me spinning: *"what's the one thing?"*

**What I need from the team:**
Authenticity. Don't mansplain. Be aware there may be cross-cultural differences in how I say things — if something lands oddly, come talk to me rather than interpreting it. I'm forgiving and I want conversations over conclusions. The door is always open.

**One thing people misread about me:**
My directness and my in-the-moment expressions. If I say "I'm tired" or "I'm feeling overwhelmed" — that is me stating my current state, not a signal to take work away from me or panic. It's honest self-reporting, not a cry for help. Also: I can sound blunt when I'm moving fast. It's not personal, it's pace. If something I said landed hard, come ask me about it before you decide what it meant.

## QUESTION SET B — PROTAGONIST DEEP DIVE
*Two question sets merged and deduplicated: Stage 2 Deep Questionnaire (Ch. 7B) + Protagonist Zoom Interview Questions (from deck).*
*Sent to shortlisted candidates only (8–10 people). NOT all 30.*
*Also used as the framework for the 60-minute Zoom interviews with the 5 confirmed protagonists.*
*Status: AWAITING Munish approval before interviews begin.*

> **TEAM REVIEW INSTRUCTIONS:** Read through. Suggest additions, removals, or edits inline. Format: `> SUGGESTED: [your suggestion] — [your name] [date]`. Flag duplicates or questions that feel redundant. Do not delete — flag only.

---

### OPENING — Identity & Starting Point

**1.** Before we start — how are you feeling right now, on the eve of building something you've never built before?

**2.** Tell me who you are. Not your job title — who you actually are, and how you ended up here.

**3.** Describe your life right now — not your job title, your actual life. What's hard? What's good? What's unresolved?

**4.** What did the people in your life say when you told them what you were doing?

---

### THE DECISION & THE REASON

**5.** Why did you actually apply to this program? Not the version you'd say in a job interview — the version you'd tell a friend at 11pm.

**6.** What's the idea you're building, and where did it come from? Was there a specific moment?

**7.** Tell me everything about your app idea. Not the elevator pitch — the real version. Why this idea? Why now? Who specifically is it for?

**8.** What do you believe that most people around you don't? About technology, about yourself, about what's possible?

---

### TECHNOLOGY IDENTITY

**9.** How do you see yourself when it comes to technology — and where does that story come from? Is there a specific moment or person who shaped it?

**10.** Complete this sentence: *'I've always thought of myself as someone who is not technical because...'*

---

### DOUBT & FEAR

**11.** What are you most afraid of in the next twelve weeks? Be specific. 'Failing' is not specific enough.

**12.** Do you honestly believe you can build something in twelve weeks? What makes you doubt it?

**13.** Is there a moment you can think of when you almost stopped something before? What kept you going?

**14.** What have you tried to build or create in the past that didn't work? What happened, and what did you do with that?

---

### STAKES & PURPOSE

**15.** Who is this for — really? Is there a specific person, real or imagined, whose life this would change?

**16.** Who else is this journey for? Is there someone watching — someone you want to prove something to, or someone this is secretly dedicated to?

**17.** What would it mean — really mean — to you if this worked? And what would it mean if it didn't?

---

### THE COHORT

**18.** You're doing something hard alongside strangers from six countries. What do you make of that?

**19.** Has anyone in the cohort already surprised you?

---

### BEING FILMED

**20.** How do you feel about being filmed? About your story being part of a documentary? Is there anything you're worried about?

**21.** Is there anything about being filmed that worries you? Anything you'd want us to know before we start?

> *(Note: Q20 and Q21 cover similar ground — team to flag if one should be cut or merged)*

---

### THE BEFORE & AFTER

**22.** Describe the version of yourself that is walking into this program. Three words — then tell me why those three words.

**23.** Describe the version of yourself you are hoping to be at the end. Three more words. What would it take to get there?

**24.** What is the thing in your life right now that makes this harder than it needs to be?

---

### CLOSING

**25.** Six months from now, what do you hope you can say about this experience?

**26.** Is there anything nobody has asked you yet — about this, about you — that you wish they would?

**27.** Say your name, where you are in the world, and one word for how you feel at the start of this.
